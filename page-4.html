Indoor Plant Care 101

Dive into the world of successful indoor plant care with our best tips for mastering the skills needed to nurture vibrant and thriving houseplants!
Whether you're a novice enthusiast or an experienced gardener, grasping the fundamental principles of caring for houseplants is the key to cultivating a lush and inviting indoor haven. Explore the core aspects of tending to your leafy companions, and gain new insights and techniques that will ensure your plants will not only endure, but thrive. From understanding light and watering requirements to managing humidity levels and perfecting the art of pruning, our expert-approved tips will empower you to curate a stunning collection of plants that infuse serenity and vitality into your living spaces.

Quick Tips

Lighting

Proper lighting is crucial for indoor plants. Different plants have varying light requirements, so it's important to understand your plant's needs. Generally, place high-light plants closer to windows with ample sunlight, while low-light plants can thrive in shady spots. Rotate your plants periodically to ensure even growth as they reach for the light.


Watering

Overwatering is a common pitfall. Be sure to water your plants appropriately, considering factors like plant type, pot size, and environmental conditions. Allow the top inch of soil to dry before watering again. Using pots with drainage holes can help prevent waterlogging. Remember, it's better to under water slightly than to drown your plants.


Humidity

Indoor environments can sometimes be dry, especially in heated or air-conditioned spaces. Some plants, such as tropical varieties, thrive in higher humidity. You can increase humidity by misting your plants, placing them on trays filled with water and pebbles, or using a room humidifier.


Potting & Soil

Choosing the right potting mix is essential. Use well-draining soil appropriate for your plant type. Repot your plants when they outgrow their containers, providing them with fresh nutrients and space for growth.


Fertilizing

Indoor plants benefit from occasional fertilizing during their active growing seasons (usually spring and summer). Use a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer and follow the instructions on the packaging. Avoid over-fertilizing, as this can harm the plant.


Pruning & Grooming

Regularly prune your plants to remove dead or yellowing leaves, encourage bushier growth, and maintain their overall shape. Grooming also helps improve airflow around the plant, reducing the risk of pests and diseases.
